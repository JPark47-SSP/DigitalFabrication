Project Overview

The Star Family Sculpture is a kinetic art piece designed to combine mechanical movement with artistic expression. It features moving parts controlled by embedded circuitry, creating a dynamic visual experience inspired by the concept of family and stars.

Materials and Tools Used
  • Arduino Uno microcontroller
  • Servo motors (3x)
  • Laser-cut acrylic sheets
  • Wood base and structural supports
  • Jumper wires and breadboard
  • Soldering iron and solder
  • 3D-printed connectors and joints
  • Power supply (5V)
  • Various screws, nuts, and bolts
  • Hand tools: screwdriver, wire stripper, pliers
Circuitry and Electronics

The sculpture's movement is controlled by three servo motors connected to an Arduino Uno. The Arduino is programmed to rotate the motors in a synchronized pattern, simulating the movement of stars orbiting around a center. The wiring is done on a breadboard with proper power management to ensure stable operation.

Key Components:

  • Arduino Uno for control logic
  • Servo motors connected to digital PWM pins
  • External 5V power supply for motors
  • Push button for manual start/stop control

Challenges and Learning Reflections

During the fabrication of this sculpture, the main challenges were synchronizing the servo movements smoothly and designing mechanical joints that balanced flexibility and stability. Learning to integrate electronic control with mechanical design deepened my understanding of kinetic art and embedded systems.

Future improvements may include adding sensors for interactive control and experimenting with different materials to enhance aesthetics and durability.
